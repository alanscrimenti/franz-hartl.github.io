Subjects' ratings of praise and blame were assessed on a scale from 0 (no praise or blame) to 6 (a lot of praise or blame).

On this scale, the mean level of blame for hitting the aunt by luck was 5.4 (almost the maximum possible blame).

By contrast, the mean level of praise for hitting the bull's-eye by luck was only 3.4 (close to the half-way point).

When we collapse across conditions, there is a correlation between people's tendency to assign a lot of praise or blame and people's tendency to consider the behavior intentional, r = .54, p < .001.
