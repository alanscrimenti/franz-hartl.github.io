Explaining the Results

Why do we obtain these results? Once again, it seems plausible to suppose that people consider a behavior intentional when the agent stands toward that behavior in a relation such that the agent could be blamed for it (if the behavior is bad) or praised for it (if the behavior is good).

Thus, if the agent manages to hit the bull's-eye by sheer luck, it seems that the agent doesn't deserve much praise for hitting the bull's-eye. But if the agent hits his aunt by pure luck, he still deserves a great deal of blame for hitting her. And perhaps that's why people say that the agent hit the aunt intentionally but didn't hit the bull's-eye intentionally. (show statistics)
