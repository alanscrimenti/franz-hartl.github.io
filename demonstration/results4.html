Results

Most subjects agreed with the claim that the chairman harmed the environment intentionally. (show statistics)

In other words, subjects seem not to have classified the chairman's behavior neither as intentional nor as unintentional. They seem to have classified it as truly intentional.

Conversely, when subjects were told that the chairman helped the environment, most agreed with the claim that this behavior was unintentional. (show statistics) So these subjects seem to have classified the behavior as truly unintentional.

This result helps to address a long-standing philosophical question. Some philosophers say that side-effects are always regarded as intentional; some that side-effects are always regarded as unintentional; and some that side-effects are regarded neither as intentional nor as unintentional.

The present results seem to indicate that all of these views are overly simplistic. The truth is that some side-effects are regarded as intentional and others as unintentional, with people's intuitions varying depending on whether the side-effect itself is felt to be good or bad.
