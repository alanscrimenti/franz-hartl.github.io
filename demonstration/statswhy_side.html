Subjects' ratings of praise and blame were assessed on a scale from 0 (no praise or blame) to 6 (a lot of praise or blame).

On this scale, the mean level of blame for harming the environment was 5.3 (almost the maximum possible blame).

By contrast, the mean level of praise for helping the environment was only 2.1.

When we collapse across conditions, there is a correlation between people's tendency to assign a lot of praise or blame and people's tendency to consider the behavior intentional, r = .61, p < .001.
