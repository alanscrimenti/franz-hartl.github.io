EXPERIMENTAL PHILOSOPHY LAB

 
 


The Experimental Philosophy Lab is an interdisciplinary team, composed of psychologists and philosophers, working together to address questions of common interest. Research in the lab focuses especially on the fundamental cognitive processes underlying people's intuitions about matters of traditional philosophical concern. So the lab's research is concerned with the sorts of questions that have been traditionally associated with philosophy (morality, free will, etc.), but it approaches these questions using the sorts of methods characteristic of contemporary cognitive science (experimental studies, statistical analysis, etc.).

At the moment, researchers within the lab are conducting experimental studies on questions about free will and the self, about moral relativism, about causation by omission, and about numerous other topics at the intersection of philosophy and psychology. 

If you are a graduate student interested in joining the lab, please feel free to contact the lab director Joshua Knobe. Or, if you just want to learn a little bit more about experimental philosophy generally, you can check out the Experimental Philosophy Page.
